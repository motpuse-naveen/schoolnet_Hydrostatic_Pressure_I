Aim: To determine the density of an
unknown liquid using a U-tube
Manometer.

Apparatus
U-tube Manometer with one tube
attached to a flexible rubber tube

Funnel having mouth sealed with a
flexible rubber diaphragm

Jar

Liquids of different densities, such as
mercury, water, glycerine, oil etc.

Material:
Water

Procedure
Lower the funnel for some distance
below the liquid surface in the jar.

Note down the depth at which the funnel
is positioned below the liquid surface.

Note the difference in height between
the liquid in the two arms of the
manometer.

Calculate the density of the liquid
using the equation provided.


Repeat the experiment with a different liquid.

Calculations:
Manometer Readings:

Height difference between the two liquid arms of manometer, H1(cm)

Density of liquid in the Manometer, D1(gm/cc)

Jar Readings:

Depth of funnel below liquid level in the Jar, H2 (cm)

Density of liquid in the Jar, D2: (gm/cc)

Pressure due to liquid column in
Manometer = Pressure due to liquid
column in Jar

H1 x D1 x g = H2 x D2 x g (where g is acceleration due to gravity)
